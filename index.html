Dataset for the Research of Wireless Performance of Sub-1 GHz Networks

Presented here is a dataset used for our research of Wireless Performance of Sub-1 GHz Networks. The dataset was recorded using the experimental setups described in [1]. The purpose of our testbed was to explore the influence of practical environments and provide a checklist of potential factors affecting wireless network performance in realistic environments. The figure blow shows the Drone Modules and Ground Modules we used throughout the experiments.


Drone Modules (a) and Ground Modules (b)

We set up controlled experiments in laboratory (indoor) and outdoor environments. Five experimental scenarios were arranged: Indoor, Street, Open-field, Ground-to-Drone (G2D) and Drone-to-Drone (D2D). Digi XBee-PRO 900HP modules [2], from the Digi family, was selected for the end-to-end wireless network. With the coordinator and the configuration software, XBee Configuration & Test Utility (XCTU), we could remotely change the settings of the transmitter during the tests. Detailed setups for specific scenarios can be found in [1]. The figure blow shows the general setup.


General Setup

In this study, our focus was to initiate the checklist of realistic parameters in the context of Sub-1 GHz wireless networks. The experiments were implemented with indoor and urban outdoor scenarios. The result shows that the wireless signal propagation is generally affected by the characteristics of the medium of the line of transmission (distance, path, surrounding) [3], hardware configuration, and qualitative conditions such as weather and hardware installation. we categorize them into four general categories: path, configuration, weather, and installation. A detailed checklist can be found in [1]. The table below shows the categores and factors from the checklist.
